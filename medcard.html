Digital Decision-Making Aids for Depression

Decision-making, Healthcare, Doctor-patient interaction
Project Intro

This project was finished in Spring 2016, where I worked with Professor Nassim Jafarinaimi and two other professionals from Mayo Clinic.

Design Goal

Through digital medication cards, we are trying to build pleasant doctor-patient relationships, facilitate conversation, and accelerate decision making.

Deliverables

We created a fully functional prototype demonstrating key features, and a process book documenting each steps as well.

Identifying Current Problems

We had a brief talk with the designer of paper cards and two doctors who use them.
Scalability

Due to the high cost of printing, doctors/patients are not allowed to write/take notes on the card in order to reuse, which is not handy.

Modularity

When new medication comes out, which is not rare to see, Mayo Clinic has to update information and reprint cards, which is is costly.

Usability

Even though current cards work fine, there is still a number of problems needed to be solved regarding the usabilities.

Creating Journey Map

Taking those problems in mind, we start understanding users' perceptions and usability issues.

Prototyping - Round One

Based on the journey map, we created following functionalities & Screens.

Starter - Introducing need-to-knows

Introduction - Introducing each issue and how they work out

Comparison & Auto-selection - Doctors and patients work together to find the proper medication(s), by setting up filters for each issue.

Summary - Summarizing medications, displaying specific side effects, and sharing/printing it out

Prototyping - Round Two

We took screens to class, showed them to professors/classmates, and asked for feedbacks.

Starter - Visually more readable and cagetorized

Introduction - Adding explanations to each issue

Comparison & Manual-selection - Removing filters and enabling user selections

Summary - Users could select whichever/multiple medication(s) to print out.
